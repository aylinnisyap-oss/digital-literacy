

## Case Study: Ransomware Attack on a Hospital System**

Ransomware is a type of cybercrime where attackers block access to a system or data and demand payment (usually in cryptocurrency) to restore it. 
A well-known example is the **WannaCry attack (2017)**, which affected hospitals, businesses, and governments worldwide.

In a typical ransomware attack, the process begins when a user unknowingly clicks on a malicious email link or downloads an infected attachment.
This installs malware into the system. Next, the ransomware spreads across the network, encrypting files and locking critical data. Once encryption is complete, a message appears demanding payment in exchange for a decryption key. Victims are often given a deadline, increasing panic and pressure.

Hospitals, small businesses, and government organizations are common targets because they rely heavily on immediate access to data. In the WannaCry incident, several hospitals in the UK had to cancel surgeries and turn away patients because their systems were locked.

The consequences of ransomware attacks are severe. Organizations face financial losses, operational shutdowns, and damage to reputation. 
Sensitive data may also be leaked or permanently lost. In critical sectors like healthcare, such attacks can even risk human lives.

This case highlights the importance of cybersecurity awareness, regular software updates, and data backups to prevent and mitigate ransomware threats.
